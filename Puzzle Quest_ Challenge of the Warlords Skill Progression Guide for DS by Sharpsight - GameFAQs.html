= 2. Introduction            (PQINT) =
======================================

Puzzle Quest has a fairly confusing skill advancement system, with different
skills advancing at non-linear rates. I was trying to figure out the ideal
advancement strategy, and constructed the table included in this guide as a tool
to simplify progress analysis.

Additionally, in constructing this table, I had to come up with a strategy for
quickly raising skill levels. A brief summary of the process I used is included
as well. Please note that this game becomes VERY EASY once you start farming
skills. I HIGHLY recommend that you only farm skills for competitive multiplayer
purposes, as farming trivializes the main story.

The table of stat progression, as far as I know, has not been published
elsewhere online. (At least, so far as my limited Google queries could find!)
I hope this information is useful to a few other min/maxers out there.

======================================
= 3. Skill Basics            (PQBAS) =
======================================

In Puzzle Quest, there are two main avenues of character development. First,
there is equipment, which is not covered in this guide. However, the bulk of
your progress comes from increasing skill points.

Skill points can be acquired either by spending skill points achieved from
levelling up, or through purchase at the citadel (after a Temple has been
built).

Each skill is aligned with a different type of gem:

============================
    Skill     |  Gem Type
============================
Earth Mastery | Green Gems
Fire Mastery  | Red Gems
Air Mastery   | Yellow Gems
Water Mastery | Blue Gems
Battle        | Skull
Cunning       | Gold
Morale        | Purple Stars

The different skills also affect different attributes:

========================
= i) Elemental Mastery =
========================

The elemental mastery skills only affect mana and gems of the appropriate
colour.

Basic stat as listed in the Skill Progress Table:
x% bonus to mana from matching gems.

Other effects:
x bonus to maximum mana.
x bonus to starting mana.
x% chance of an extra turn when matching gems.
x% chance of a wildcard when matching gems.

==============
= ii) Battle =
==============

Battle affects Skulls and inflicted damage.

Basic stat as listed in the Skill Progress Table:
x% bonus to inflicted damage (from all sources).

Other effects:
x% chance of an extra turn when matching skulls.
x% chance of a wildcard when matching skulls.

================
= iii) Cunning =
================

Cunning affects Gold and Wildcards.

Basic stat as listed in the Skill Progress Table:
x% bonus to wildcard effects.
x% bonus to experience/gold earned from defeated foes.

Other effects:
x% chance of an extra turn when matching gold.
x% chance of a wildcard when matching gold.

==============
= iv) Morale =
==============

Morale affects Purple Stars and Life Points.

Basic stat as listed in the Skill Progress Table:
*No basic stat

Other effects:
x bonus to life points.
x% bonus to all spell resistance.
x% chance of an extra turn when matching stars.
x% chance of a wildcard when matching stars.

= 4. Skill Progression Table (PQTAB) =
======================================

In order to limit duplicate information, this table is divided into four
sections:

For clarification of which stats are affected by which skills, as well as the
appropriate colour for each gem, please refer to the PQBAS section.

==============================
= i) Basic/Common Attributes =
==============================

Rnk  = Rank
Turn = x% chance of an extra turn when matching gems of the appropriate colour.
Wild = x% chance of a wildcard when matching gems of the appropriate colour.
Base = x% bonus to the appropriate "basic stat".

=====================================
= ii) Elemental Specific Attributes =
=====================================

Max   = x bonus to maximum mana of the appropriate colour.
Start = x bonus to starting mana of the appropriate colour.

===================================
= iii) Morale Specific Attributes =
===================================

HP  = x bonus to life points.
Res = x% bonus to all spell resistance.

======================
= iv) Training Costs =
======================

Cost     = n gold required to train this rank
Ttl Cost = n gold required to train up to this rank (starting from 0).

= 5. A Note on Accuracy      (PQACC) =
======================================

There are two caveats to the accuracy of the above table:

i) Rounding:

In many cases I used Excel formulas to calculate the above values, and Excel
always rounds up, but Puzzle Quest is inconsistent in their rounding; sometimes
rounding up and sometimes rounding down. Accordingly all (non-even) values
presented in the above table may be off by +/- 0.01% of what's displayed in
game.

ii) Game Version:

All the above stats were based on the Nintendo DS version of Puzzle Quest. I
assume the values are the same in other versions of the game, but I have not
attempted to verify this claim. If anyone with another version of the game finds
major errors (i.e. not a rounding mistake), please contact me and I will update
this section of the guide to clarify.

= 6. Skill Farming           (PQFRM) =
======================================

WARNING: SKILL FARMING TRIVIALIZES THE STORY MODE OF THIS GAME. DO NOT CONTINUE
READING IF YOU HAVE NOT YET BEAT THIS GAME AT LEAST ONCE UNLESS YOU'RE A DIRTY
CHEATER!

WARNING: SKILLS RESET AT 256 GOING BACK TO 0. BE ESPECIALLY CAREFUL, SINCE SOME
QUESTS PERMANENTLY INCREASE YOUR STATS, WHICH COULD SEND YOU BACK TO 0.

Skill farming is a fairly contentious issue: Essentially, you farm gold which
you the use to purchase skill upgrades from your citadel's temple.

For a more detailed explanation of skill farming, please refer to mikedn8c's
Wizard Guide. I only want to add a route which I found much more efficient.

mikedn8c's guide:
http://www.gamefaqs.com/portable/ds/file/934598/48860

WHEN RIDING A SLOW MOUNT, it takes 8 days to go between Bartonia and Enmoth,
9 days to go between Bartonia and The Trarg, and 9 days to go between Enmouth
and The Trarg. Thus, going in the cycle: Bartonia -> Enmoth -> The Trarg takes
26 days, so almost every cycle you'll get gold from each city.

Please note that riding a slow mount is required. This is the fastest way of
gaining gold in Puzzle Quest since mount speed affects the in-game time it takes
to travel between locations, but NOT the real-life time it takes. Thus, the
slower the mount, the quicker (irl) the month will reset.
